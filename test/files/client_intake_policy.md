# Silverthorne Legal Group – Client Intake Policy (2025)

## 1. Purpose

This document describes the official client intake process at Silverthorne Legal Group (“SLG”).  
The process ensures that all new matters comply with ethical rules, conflict-of-interest checks, and proper documentation standards.

## 2. Initial Inquiry

All initial inquiries, whether by phone, email, or website form, must be logged in the SLG CRM system within 24 hours.

The following information must be collected:
- Full name of the potential client.
- Basic description of the legal issue.
- Names of all opposing or related parties.
- Preferred contact method.
- Urgency assessment (“Routine”, “Time-Sensitive”, “Emergency”).

## 3. Conflict Check

### 3.1 Procedure

Before any legal advice is given, a mandatory conflict check must be performed using the Firm Conflict Database.

The conflict check must include:
1. Names of the potential client.
2. Names of adverse or related parties.
3. Any attorneys at SLG with prior involvement in similar matters.

### 3.2 Outcomes

A conflict check can result in three outcomes:
- **Clear** → Proceed with intake.
- **Conditional Approval** → Consult with the Managing Partner before continuing.
- **Denied** → Decline representation and provide a standard referral list.

No attorney may proceed with representation without a documented conflict clearance.

## 4. Required Documents

Before opening a formal matter, the following must be obtained:
- Signed Engagement Agreement.
- Copy of government-issued identification.
- Completed Client Information Form.
- Retainer payment or approved alternative billing arrangement.

## 5. Matter Opening

Once documentation is complete, the Client Services Coordinator opens the matter in the Practice Management System.

The matter must be assigned:
- A matter number.
- A responsible attorney.
- A billing category (“Hourly”, “Flat-Fee”, or “Contingency”).

## 6. Time Expectations

- Initial inquiries must be logged within **24 hours**.
- Conflict checks must be completed within **1 business day**.
- Matter opening should occur within **2 business days** after all documents are received.

## 7. Review Schedule

This policy is reviewed annually by the Managing Partner and updated as needed.
