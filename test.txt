
# silverthorne legal group – associate attorney onboarding guide (2025)

this onboarding guide applies to all new associate attorneys at silverthorne legal group (“slg”).

## 1. first week – essentials

during the first week, new associates must:

1. set up accounts for:
   - slg email
   - document management system (dms)
   - timekeeping and billing system
   - legal research platform (lexisearch)
2. attend mandatory trainings:
   - ethics and confidentiality (1 hour)
   - timekeeping best practices (45 minutes)
   - conflict-check training (30 minutes)
3. shadow a senior attorney in at least one client meeting.

## 2. first month – integration

during the first month, associates should:

- review the **client intake policy (2025)**.
- observe at least **one deposition or hearing**.
- draft:
  - one research memo.
  - one client communication under supervision.
- submit all billable time by **9:00 am each weekday**.

a check-in with the practice group leader is required at the end of week 4.

## 3. first 90 days – competency goals

by day 90, associates are expected to:

- perform conflict checks independently.
- open a matter in the practice management system (with oversight).
- demonstrate understanding of:
  - annual leave policy
  - sick leave policy
  - cle budget rules
- assist in at least **two client matters**.

## 4. evaluation

the probationary review (90 days) assesses:

- legal analysis
- writing quality
- responsiveness to clients
- use of the dms
- adherence to slg procedures

# Silverthorne Legal Group – Client Intake Policy (2025)

## 1. Purpose

This document describes the official client intake process at Silverthorne Legal Group (“SLG”).  
The process ensures that all new matters comply with ethical rules, conflict-of-interest checks, and proper documentation standards.

## 2. Initial Inquiry

All initial inquiries, whether by phone, email, or website form, must be logged in the SLG CRM system within 24 hours.

The following information must be collected:
- Full name of the potential client.
- Basic description of the legal issue.
- Names of all opposing or related parties.
- Preferred contact method.
- Urgency assessment (“Routine”, “Time-Sensitive”, “Emergency”).

## 3. Conflict Check

### 3.1 Procedure

Before any legal advice is given, a mandatory conflict check must be performed using the Firm Conflict Database.

The conflict check must include:
1. Names of the potential client.
2. Names of adverse or related parties.
3. Any attorneys at SLG with prior involvement in similar matters.

### 3.2 Outcomes

A conflict check can result in three outcomes:
- **Clear** → Proceed with intake.
- **Conditional Approval** → Consult with the Managing Partner before continuing.
- **Denied** → Decline representation and provide a standard referral list.

No attorney may proceed with representation without a documented conflict clearance.

## 4. Required Documents

Before opening a formal matter, the following must be obtained:
- Signed Engagement Agreement.
- Copy of government-issued identification.
- Completed Client Information Form.
- Retainer payment or approved alternative billing arrangement.

## 5. Matter Opening

Once documentation is complete, the Client Services Coordinator opens the matter in the Practice Management System.

The matter must be assigned:
- A matter number.
- A responsible attorney.
- A billing category (“Hourly”, “Flat-Fee”, or “Contingency”).

## 6. Time Expectations

- Initial inquiries must be logged within **24 hours**.
- Conflict checks must be completed within **1 business day**.
- Matter opening should occur within **2 business days** after all documents are received.

## 7. Review Schedule

This policy is reviewed annually by the Managing Partner and updated as needed.

# Silverthorne Legal Group – Employee Handbook (2025)

## 1. Working Hours and Expectations

Standard business hours are **8:30 AM to 5:30 PM, Monday through Friday**.  
Employees are expected to be available during core hours: **10:00 AM to 3:00 PM**.

Overtime for non-exempt employees must be approved in advance by a supervisor.

## 2. Leave Policies

### 2.1 Annual Leave

Full-time employees receive **20 days of paid annual leave** per calendar year.  
A maximum of **5 unused days may be carried over** to the next year.

Annual leave requests must be submitted at least **7 days in advance**.

### 2.2 Sick Leave

Employees receive **8 paid sick days** per year.  
Absences longer than **2 consecutive days** require a doctor’s note.

Unused sick leave does **not** carry over.

### 2.3 Court Leave

Employees summoned for jury duty or subpoenaed as a witness will receive paid court leave for the duration of their required service.

## 3. Professional Development

SLG offers an annual **$1,500 Continuing Legal Education (CLE) budget** for each attorney.  
Unused CLE funds do not carry over.

## 4. Performance Reviews

All employees receive:
- A probationary review at 90 days.
- Annual performance reviews in January.

Reviews evaluate:
- Professional competence.
- Client communication.
- Adherence to deadlines.
- Team collaboration.

## 5. Code of Conduct

Employees must:
- Maintain client confidentiality.
- Avoid conflicts of interest.
- Report ethical concerns to the Ethics Committee.
- Treat colleagues and clients with respect at all times.

Violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination.
